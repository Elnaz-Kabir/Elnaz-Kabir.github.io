About Me

I'm an Assistant Professor in the Department of Engineering Technology & Industrial Distribution (ETID) at Texas A&M University. In my research, I am interested in developing data-driven frameworks under uncertainty in order to address the resiliency challenges of critical infrastructures, in particular power systems. My research integrates predictive and prescriptive analytics, drawing on methodologies from data science, machine learning, risk analysis, simulation, stochastic and robust optimization. My current projects include applications in a broad range of power system resiliency areas including energy system dicarbonization, and power outage management. Before joining ETID, I was a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at Cornell University. I received my Ph.D. degree in Industrial and Operations Engineering and my M.S. degree in Statistics, both from University of Michigan.
